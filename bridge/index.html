Complex Numbers, bridge example

Martin Clark and Gary Ballantyne
May 2016

[This web page is currently under development.]

Complex numbers appear often in science and engineering. The suspension of your car, the electronics in your phone, skyscrapers and bridges all use complex numbers in their design.

However, the most important (and interesting) examples are advanced—so, when you start learning about complex numbers it can appear that they have little practical use. This is far from true.

Differential equations are just about the most powerful mathematical tool available. By themselves, they are too advanced for us today. But, with complex numbers we can transform a differential equation into a (much easier) quadratic equation.

Extending Eulers equation

From Eulers equation you know that for an imaginary number $ib$ $$ e^{ib} = \cos b + i\sin b $$ Its a small step so consider a complex number $a+ib$ $$ e^{a+ib} = e^{a}e^{ib} = e^{a} \left( \cos b + i\sin b \right)$$ And, just a hop to multiply the complex number by time, $t$ $$ e^{st} = e^{(a+ib)t} = e^{at}e^{ibt} = e^{at} \left( \cos bt + i\sin bt \right)$$ It mightn't look like much, $e^{st}$ is the most important solution to the most important class of differential equations (which are the most important tool in all of mathematics). All you need to do is work out what $s$ is.

Eigenstuff

If you know anything about the function $f(x)=e^x$, you know that differention doesn't affect it. Now, if you know the chain-rule $$\frac{d}{dt} e^{st} = se^{st}$$ Lets consider our first (and the simplest) differential equation: $$ \frac{dy}{dt}=0 $$ To solve it, we assume $e^{st}$ is a solution for some value of $s$. To find $s$, substitute $y=e^{st}$ above to find $$ se^{st} = 0 $$ You can easily see that this equation is solved by $s=0$, so a solution is $$ y = e^{st} = e^{0t} = 1 $$

Bridge

Although we only solved a very simple differential equation, the recipe can be applied to much more interesting problems. Including the design of bridges. First watch this short video. The design of this bridge used the wrong complex number.

The equation for the angle of twist ($\phi$) of the bridge is $$ \frac{d^2\phi}{t^2} + 2\zeta\frac{d\phi}{dt} + \phi=0 $$ The equation has just one parameter $\zeta$ (zeta) for the engineer to design. The problem is, what value should she choose? We can solve this equation with the same recipe as above: (1) assume a $\phi=e^{st}$ is a solution; (2) substitute into the differential equation to find an equation for $s$; (3) solve that equation for $s$. Step 1: $$ \phi = e^{st} $$ Step 2: $$ s^2\cancel{e^{st}}+2\zeta s \cancel{e^{st}} + \cancel{e^{st}} = 0 $$ Step 3: $$ s = -\zeta \pm \sqrt{\zeta^2-1} $$ Now, with hardly any further maths, we can see what happened to the bridge. Say $\zeta$ is less than zero and greater than -1 ($-1<\zeta<0$). Then the square-root value is imaginary part of $s$ (which we called $b$ above) and the real part of $s$ is $\zeta$ ($a$ above), which is positive. $$ y = e^{st} = e^{at}e^{ibt} $$
